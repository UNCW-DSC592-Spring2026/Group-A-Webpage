Component diagram

Internal data flow

The component diagram shows how raw app reviews flow through the system's internal stages — from nightly ingestion and categorization through text cleaning, ML modeling, and final dashboard delivery — with credential-based access control at both storage and visualization layers.

Key components

What each stage does

1

Nightly refresh & scraping

A scheduled nightly trigger fires the Review Scraper, which collects new app store reviews and feeds them as raw data into the pipeline before the business day begins.

2

Review categorization

Raw reviews are categorized by type. The Predictive Review Model can also inject predicted reviews triggered by outage detection events, enriching the input set proactively.

3

Data splitting & text cleaning

Reviews are split into substantive and non-substantive groups. Both streams pass through text cleaning and normalization — removing noise, stopwords, and inconsistencies.

4

Sentiment & topic labeling

Cleaned reviews are labeled with sentiment (positive, negative, neutral) and topic categories. Processed data is simultaneously written to on-site storage via the Credential Authenticator.

5

ML pipeline

Labeled and processed data flows into the ML pipeline, which generates predicted ratings and model results for each review.

6

Dashboard & access control

ML results and labeled data are surfaced through a filterable dashboard and visualization layer, both protected by a Credential Authenticator for secure internal access.
