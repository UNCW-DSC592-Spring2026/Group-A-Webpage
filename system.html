Architecture & design

How the App Review Rating Prediction System is structured, how data flows through it, and how its components interact to deliver nightly intelligence to your team.

System context

Context diagram

The context diagram defines the system boundary and shows who interacts with the system and what information flows in and out — without exposing internal implementation details.

Feedback loop

The gold dashed arrow represents the system's iterative learning nature — accumulated historical data and past predictions are used to periodically retrain the model and refine categorization logic over time. The system is designed to improve as more review data becomes available.

Component diagram

Internal data flow

The component diagram shows how raw app reviews flow through the system's internal stages — from ingestion and prioritization through processing, ML modeling, and final dashboard delivery — including access control for both technicians and end users.

Key components

What each stage does

1

Data organization & prioritization

Raw reviews are split into substantive and non-substantive groups. Reviews with meaningful textual feedback are prioritized for deeper analysis.

2

Processing & cleaning

Text is normalized, stopwords removed, and duplicates filtered. Both review streams are merged into a clean, consistent dataset.

3

Labeling

Processed reviews are labeled with sentiment (positive, negative, neutral), issue categories, and other analytical tags.

4

On-site storage

Processed and labeled data is persisted to internal storage, ensuring consistency across nightly refresh cycles.

5

ML pipeline

The trained model scores each review, generating predicted ratings and surfacing high-priority items for analyst attention.

6

Dashboard & access control

Results are delivered through a filterable dashboard. Both technicians and users authenticate via credentials before accessing the system.
