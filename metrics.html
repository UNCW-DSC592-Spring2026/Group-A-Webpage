Key finding

Model performance improves substantially as we move from linear to tree-based to gradient-boosting methods. The base linear regression model performs the worst, with a negative R² indicating it fails to capture meaningful structure in the data. LightGBM achieves the lowest RMSE (0.1958) and MAE (0.1366) and the highest R² (0.4344) — making it the strongest model for this prediction task. Further preprocessing experimentation may improve performance further.

Review length & trends over time

Most reviews are short — the length distribution is right-skewed, with a small number of long, detailed reviews. Review volume shows an upward trend from 2009–2017 with a sharp drop around September 2017. Average ratings peaked early (~0.97 in 2009) and declined to their lowest around 2015–2016 (~0.79) before a modest recovery.
